The Man Who Sees
What Others Walk Past

A portrait of Port Moody's most tenacious real estate advocate — and the quiet conviction that drives him.

There is a particular kind of clarity that comes from knowing exactly why you do what you do. For Dawar Zada, that clarity arrived not in a boardroom or on a conference call — but in the back yards and living rooms of Port Moody, listening to families who had built their lives on land that was quietly worth far more than they realized.

Dawar grew up understanding that real estate is not a transaction. It is a turning point. The families he works with — homeowners who have spent decades tending properties in Heritage Mountain, College Park, Port Moody Centre — are not looking for a quick sale. They are looking for someone who will sit across the kitchen table, take the time to understand their full picture, and tell them the truth about what their options actually are.

That is what he does. And he does it better than almost anyone practicing in the Tri-Cities today.

As Team Lead at Zada Group, operating under Stonehaus Realty Corp., Dawar has built a practice defined by a single conviction: that the right lot, at the right time, with the right strategy, can change a family's financial future forever. It is a belief that has shaped every consultation, every listing, every recommendation he has made over a career that earned him recognition as a Master Medallion Club REALTOR® — an acknowledgment reserved for the top tier of the profession.

But recognition, to Dawar, is a byproduct. The work itself is the point. And the work right now is Port Moody Multiplex — a specialized practice he founded to help homeowners navigate BC's Bill 44 Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing legislation, which now allows 3 to 6 homes to be built on most single-family lots in the city, as of right, without rezoning. It is one of the most significant shifts in residential land use in British Columbia's history, and most people who stand to benefit from it don't yet know it applies to them.

Dawar intends to change that. One homeowner at a time.

"Most homeowners in Port Moody are sitting on a multiplex lot. They just haven't been told yet."

The Framework

Three Paths.
One Conversation.

Every homeowner's situation is different. Dawar's framework distills the possibilities into three clear strategies — so you know exactly what you're deciding.

01

Build & Hold

Develop your lot into a multiplex and retain ownership. Build long-term rental income, expand your portfolio, and let the asset appreciate under the new density rules.

02

Build & Sell

Develop the property, then sell the completed units. Realize the full uplift of the rezoning value — turn a single-family lot into a multifamily return.

03

Sell the Lot

You don't have to build. Sell your development-ready parcel to a builder or developer at a price that reflects the full multiplex potential — often significantly above standard market value.
