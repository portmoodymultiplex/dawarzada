About Dawar
"I've never been a 'this sale' guy. I want to know where you want to end up — then build the road to get you there."

Dawar has spent his career in residential real estate across Metro Vancouver. But the part that became a genuine passion wasn't the transactions – it was what they could add up to. Early in his career he noticed a pattern. Clients who bought carefully, sold at the right moment, and rolled their equity into the next step were quietly building something that looked impossible when they started.

That insight became his signature. He'd sit down with a new client, understand the real goal – not just the next purchase, but the ten-year picture – and design a strategy around it. A $400K condo becomes a townhouse becomes a detached home. Each step planned before you take it. Each sale timed to maximise what you carry into the next one.

Over the years, those first-wave clients became real estate success stories. Working-class Canadians – people like his own family – who started with what they could afford and ended up with the kind of wealth they never expected to build. That track record, and what it meant for those families, is what drew him immediately to BC's new SSMUH legislation the moment it passed.

For a Port Moody homeowner who had already made those smart moves and landed on a detached lot, the multiplex rules are a completely new chapter – turning one property into generational income without selling what they'd worked decades to build. Port Moody Multiplex is the practice Dawar built around that opportunity.

The Wealth Roadmap

How Dawar's clients build wealth

This pattern has repeated itself across Dawar's career. It starts with whatever you can afford. It ends somewhere you didn't think was possible.

1
Start with what you can afford

A condo. An entry-level townhouse. Doesn't matter. The point isn't perfection – it's entering the market with a plan already built around where you want to end up.

2
Build equity with intention

Dawar designs a strategy around your property from day one. When to hold. When to improve. What signals to watch. When to sell. Nothing improvised.

3
Sell at the right moment

Timing matters more than most people realise. His record buys and sales aren't luck – they're the result of knowing the market and negotiating without flinching.

4
Roll it into the next step

Condo proceeds become a townhouse down payment. Townhouse equity gets you into detached. Each move planned before you make it, so you're never scrambling.

5
Arrive at the goal

Some clients are three steps in. Some are eight. The endpoint looks different for everyone – but it always starts with the same honest conversation about where you actually want to land.

6
Then multiply it with SSMUH

For homeowners who've reached a Port Moody detached, BC's new multiplex rules are the next chapter – turning one lot into generational income without selling what you spent years building.
