BC Bill 44

Small-Scale Multi-Unit
Housing Legislation

In 2023, the BC Government passed Bill 44 — the Small-Scale Multi-Unit Housing (SSMUH) legislation. It fundamentally changed what homeowners are allowed to build on their lots. In most single-family zones across Port Moody, the law now permits 3 to 6 residential units as of right — meaning no rezoning application, no lengthy public hearing process, no council approval required.

You can build a multiplex on most Port Moody lots today. Not as an exception. As the rule.

This is the most significant change to residential land use in BC in a generation — and the majority of homeowners who stand to benefit from it have never been told it applies to them.

The Missing Middle

What Port Moody Has Lacked — and Why It Matters

"Missing middle" housing is the spectrum of residential density between a detached single-family home and a high-rise apartment tower — duplexes, triplexes, townhomes, coach houses, small apartment buildings. For much of the past half century, zoning rules made this kind of housing effectively illegal in most of BC's low-density neighbourhoods.

The result: entire communities with one viable housing type, escalating prices, and families priced out of the places they grew up. Port Moody felt this acutely. Bill 44 begins to correct it.

Dawar's advocacy isn't abstract. He believes that more housing types in Port Moody means more options for families, more vibrant streetscapes, and a more resilient community — without the character disruption that comes from tower-scale development.

Port Moody Multiplex

Why Dawar Built
a Whole Practice Around This

When Bill 44 passed, Dawar didn't wait. He launched Port Moody Multiplex — a standalone practice and education platform specifically designed to meet homeowners where they are: with clear information, honest options, and the expertise to execute whichever path makes sense.

The site at portmoodymultiplex.ca explains the legislation in plain language, maps eligibility across Port Moody's neighbourhoods, and walks homeowners through the three strategic paths available to them. It is education-first, transaction-second — because Dawar believes that an informed homeowner makes better decisions, and better decisions lead to better outcomes for everyone.

He has consulted with homeowners across Heritage Mountain, Heritage Woods, College Park, Port Moody Centre, Ioco, Glenayre, Barber Street, North Shore, and Seaview — covering the full geography of Port Moody's residential fabric.
